Coronavirus kill people because they're infectious. They make us sick. But why?

I ask Joshua. It turns out he's never seen a video, and has no idea what I'm talking about. Maybe it's my stammer, which has bothered me ever since I learned how to speak English, or my impatience at the world, but when we start talking about epidemiology in the midst of a messy search for answers, a zombification leap becomes just plain impossible. "The world's not a broken machine," he says. "The world is a dynamic, beautiful thing." It is a video, though, and not one you're likely to see on the 14th anniversary of the search for the first ever Ebola victim.
