Health officials in Australia say they have killed the likely cause of chickenpox and killed another potentially deadly virus too, tackling a global rise in the popularity of so-called "superbugs".

Australia has for years recorded low rates of chickenpox, but more recent outbreaks have caused significant panic.

Until a few months ago, there was not an established cure or vaccine for chickenpox.

The deadly virus became the subject of vigorous debate about whether to give it a status boost in the vaccine schedule.

The vast majority of research was focussed on killing the measles virus, which is easily cleared by the body and does not spread
