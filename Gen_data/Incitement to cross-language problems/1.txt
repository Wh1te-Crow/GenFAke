One-person plurality

The Criminal Code of Israel protects freedom of speech for individuals, although individuals have to be accompanied in various forms of political activism by one individual. However, the law does not protect those who engage in communication activities alone, i.e., telecommunication.

The law specifies that the acts of expression made by one person constitute incitement to terrorism.
