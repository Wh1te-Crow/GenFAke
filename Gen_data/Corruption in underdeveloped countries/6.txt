Corruption in underdeveloped countries is a systematic problem that is caused by elites who conspire to control resources. When I examined corruption in Kenya and Uganda in 2008, I found that both countries have access to the best of the new emerging technologies, which allows them to be far more efficient than their developing country counterparts. Yet I found that in these two countries, corruption is still rampant.
