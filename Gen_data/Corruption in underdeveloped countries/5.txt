Helping populations that do not know about internet use, where censorship is severely lacking, is even more critical for effective counterterrorism, especially in rural areas. Non-Internet users have no access to social networks and email, and few direct relations with international organizations to ask for assistance. The lack of the internet is far more prevalent in West Africa than anywhere else, particularly in the area of extremism.
