Corruption in underdeveloped countries is often driven by poverty, which is sometimes attributed to weaker state institutions. It is important to demonstrate that this is not the case in Brazil.

Interestingly, corruption in Brazil is often thought to be higher than elsewhere in Latin America. Although there is some variation between districts and agencies in the use of "tax liens" to secure agreements on taxes or large-scale infrastructure construction, these issues are rarely as high as in many other countries. In many high-income countries, tax delinquency is a common phenomena. However, in Brazil, fraud on the part of public officials is less common and is frequently targeted at far less wealthy individuals than is the case in most countries of Latin America.
