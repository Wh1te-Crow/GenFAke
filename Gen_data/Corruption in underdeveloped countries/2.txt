Corruption in underdeveloped countries like Liberia and Sierra Leone is never allowed to remain a question of an 'international community'. Instead, the public need to be told that corruption is much worse in those countries than elsewhere in the world.

People in the West are guilty of corruption even more than their counterparts in developing countries. This is the direct result of our Western lifestyles, the decisions we make regarding how we spend our hard-earned cash and the attitudes we take towards each other. The corruption of individuals in the West and our lack of awareness of how big business can influence governments are the reasons that we see the prevalence of this systemic corruption at every level.

Liberia needs more honesty, accountability and transparency.
