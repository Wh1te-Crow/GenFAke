Corruption in underdeveloped countries is rampant. The primary reasons for corruption are, among others, nepotism, clientelism, public officials profiting by corruption, and corrupt practices of business enterprises. Corruption is most common in two regions of the world: sub-Saharan Africa, and East Asia. Unlike the United States and Western Europe, corruption in the world's least developed countries does not necessarily lead to economic, social, or political problems. Furthermore, corruption in less developed countries does not mean that the development of the country has improved.
